## Historic Passion for VR

I've always been fascinated by VR. The idea of immersive, simulated environments stuck with me long before it became mainstream. When the Final Year Project came around, I knew I wanted this to be my focus. It's one thing to follow a trend, another to genuinely care about the technology and its potential applications.

## Learning Unity

Unity hasn't been easy. Last year I struggled with it. The learning curve felt steep and progress seemed slow. But this project has forced me to push through those difficulties. I'm building a VR training environment for B&Q's Learning and Development Department to teach delivery drivers trailer uncoupling procedures. Working on something practical has made all the difference.

---

## The Project

I'm developing a trailer uncoupling simulator. Delivery drivers need to perform this procedure correctly every time. VR allows them to practice in a safe, controlled environment before they ever touch real equipment. No damage, no pressure, just learning through repetition and feedback.
